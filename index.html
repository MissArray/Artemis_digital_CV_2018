A bit about me

Present

Who I am

(Among other things...)

  • A content editor and project architect who can code (CSS3, HTML5; basic JavaScript).
  • A user-oriented, competent communicator (in three languages).
  • A lateral thinker and life-long learner.

What I do

(Among other things ...)

  • I plan & deliver immersive workshops in academic writing skills in English, powered by insights from developmental copy-editing, AGILE project management and UX design.
  • I provide researchers and academic authors with tools that can help them tame unruly text, optimise their argumentation, streamline their work and convey their ideas clearly and concisely.
  • As a practising cross-disciplinarian, to build these tools I tap into the methodology of both qualitative research and user research, as well as into the principles of information architecture.
  • I also keep on training in UX design and coding in HTML5 & CSS3: I have created a number of web animations and delivered concise workshops in web design for Founders and Coders, my most recent alma mater.

What else I can do

(The list is not exhaustive...)

  • Adept at CSS3 and HTML5, Figma, Invision, Photoshop, Illustrator.
  • Well-practised at PowerPoint, Excel, Word and Google Docs.
  • Familiar with JavaScript fundamentals, Node.js, PosgtgreSQL, React, Express. I have also attended introductory workshops in Elixir and Haskell.
  • Trained in the fundamentals of UX/UI design: during the client project that is part of the graduation Founders and Coders 'Tech for Better' programme, I planned and helped conduct user-research and user tests (the latter with another team member), engaged in prototyping and liaised between the rest of the team and the clients.
  • Able to understand and take into account the needs of both users and developers.
  • Able to understand the needs of both authors and readers.
  • Speak and write in English, Greek, German.
  • Dabble with sketching and graphics (including all graphics on this site).
  • Keep on learning.

Past

I started out as a translator and evolved into a content/developmental copy editor.

Both enabled me to build a wealth of experience – but they stretched only one part of my brain.

Future

Meshing content editing and management with web design and coding offers me full brain circuit-training – and has equipped me with a versatile skill set. This includes experience in:

  • How to research, analyse, synthesise and present data.
  • How to assess the feasibility of a project.
  • How to organise and prioritise content and effort.
  • How to plan a realistic schedule and manage time and resources.
  • How to work methodically.
  • How to keep a cool head when the heat is on.
  • How to document everything.
  • How to structure arguments, research papers and websites.
  • How to handle clients.
  • How to share this knowledge through workshops and presentations.

Knowing how the cogs of a website work, how to craft the springs that lend it vigour and vibrancy and how to take forward projects that involve sharing information with users/readers is key in a range of contexts, from academic publishing to any website that relies on crisp text to reach its audience. I’m looking forward to chances to put those skills to work.

Postscript

Being active and giving back to the community matters to me.

I’m still involved with Founders and Coders: recently, I acted as one of four pastoral carers for the students of FAC16 and took part in a working group concerned with solving practical issues to do with running the course.

I have also prepared and delivered two short workshops in web design: an introduction to web typography (jointly with a fellow graduate) and an introduction to wireframes (on my own).

The intersection of art, science, technology and society is a fascinating place.

Web design and UX/UI design span all of these domains – and I'm glad I discovered it.
